This is a sample Biology textbook content about the human cell. The cell is the basic unit of life. All living organisms are made of cells. Cells can be prokaryotic (bacteria) or eukaryotic (animals and plants). Eukaryotic cells have a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. The nucleus contains DNA which carries genetic information. Mitochondria are the powerhouse of the cell, producing ATP for energy. Ribosomes synthesize proteins. The endoplasmic reticulum transports proteins and lipids. The Golgi apparatus modifies and packages proteins. Lysosomes contain digestive enzymes. The cytoplasm is the gel-like substance inside the cell. The cell membrane controls what enters and exits the cell.
